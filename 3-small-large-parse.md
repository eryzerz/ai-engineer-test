## how it's different from splitting the small vs large files
The difference is in the way we load the data, for a large file we have to implement chunking and looping instead of load the entire data directly. But this comes with a (small) cost, to do aggregation (e.g counting top subscribers countries, B2b or B2C?) we can't just do it like when we do for smaller data, since for larger data the data is splitted into several chunks we have to maintain global and local state. So, we're mapping the chunk and do aggregation by utilizing the local state, once the mapping is done, we merge it into global state, then we can present the insight of the entire data. 

There's still a catch though, if we splitted the data into chunks, it should be fine if we just want to accumulate the data, but how about doing the calculation? In `2-large-parse.ipynb`, when calculating MoM growth, I have to do separation between accumulation and growth calculation, because if I calculate a percentage change inside a chunk, then I'd only comparing the dates that exists within that specific chunk (100,000 rows) instead of the entire data. Also, a single month can be split into many chunks. So we definitely can't do the growth calculation along with the accumulation within the mapping process. The right way to do it is to accumulate the amount of subscriber per month then calculate the growth globally once the mapping and the merging is done.
